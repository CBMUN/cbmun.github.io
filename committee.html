General Assembly

DISEC

From fast-paced conflict resolution to revolutionary policy changes, the Disarmament and International Security Committee (DISEC) is at the forefront of the UN general assembly. DISEC addresses conflicts and threats to international peace and tries to maintain global stability. It is responsible for taking care of all matters related to disarmament and international security that fall within the scope of the UN charter and work towards resolving the disputes that might harm international peace and security. The committee works in close co-operation within the United Nation’s Disarmament commissions and the Geneva Based conference on disarmament If you believe in “Global Zero“ and want to make this planet a nuclear free-zone or prevent an arms race in outer space, than this year at CBMUN'19 DISEC is the committee that might electrify the interest of your mind and spark off your inner flair for international affairs!

SOCHUM

Formed after the formation of the universal declaration of human rights in 1948, SOCHUM is one of the six principal organs of UN. Commonly referred to as the "Third Committee", SOCHUM provides forum for all 193 UN member states to address issues regarding rights of women and children, treatment of refugees, international drug control, criminal justice, crime prevention, prostitution, overpopulation, social welfare, and promotion of fundamental freedoms through eradication of racism and racial discrimination. If an enthralling MUN experience is what you have been waiting for then passionate debaters and individuals eager to voice their ideas are welcome to join CBMUN'19 SOCHUM committee.

ECOSOC and Regional Body

UNCSW

United Nations Commission on the Status of Women or UNCSW for short is a commission mandated by one of UN’s main branches the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC), established on the authority of Council resolution 11(II) of 21st June 1946. Its primary purpose is to promote gender equality and to empower women across the globe. Representatives of respective member states annually conjugate at UN HQ to evaluate, formulate and execute mandates concerning gender-equality and women empowerment worldwide. UNCSW takes the leading role in promoting a gender perspective within the UN as well. The committee follows strict multi-year work programs with each year vested with its very own theme. Year 2019’s themes are as follows;
Priority theme: Social protection systems, access to public services and sustainable infrastructure for gender equality and the empowerment of women and girls.
Review theme: Women’s empowerment and the link to sustainable development, from the 60th session of the CSW.

Specialized Agencies

UNSC

United Nations Security Council is one of the six principle organs of UN with primary responsibility of maintaining international peace and security, creating conditions conductive to economic and social development while advancing universal respect for human rights. It is the only committee in UN with the ability to impose sanctions and military actions against member states in case of urgent crisis.
UNSC has an intensely unique flair given to it by veto power of the Permanent five P(5). By being the part of this committee, delegates would not only realize UNSC being the most powerful UN entity responsible for making and amending foreign policies but also the importance of the council in the governance of the UN system.
CBMUN'19 shall provide a platform for delegates to share their opinions, come forth with their solutions on what could bring about harmony, friendship, love, peace and security among these nations.

SCP

The Supreme Court of Pakistan stands as the highest jurisdictional authority in the Pakistani judicial hierarchy. Established under the authority of 8th part of the Constitution of Pakistan Supreme Court presides over and arbitrates for all other judicial courts namely high courts, district, special and Shariah courts. Being vested with the highest authority the Supreme Court is the final word in the matters of legal and constitutional disputes. Thus it also presides as the ultimate interpreter of constitutional law and it is the court of highest appeal in Pakistan. The Supreme Court consists of the Chief justice of Pakistan, sixteen justices and two ad-hoc(s) are appointed upon merit, selection and nomination of the Prime Minister and the President.

IKEAC

The Economic Advisory Council of Pakistan is a non-constitutional body organized with the primary purpose of advising the Prime Minister of Pakistan. Imran Khan, since the beginning of his term as the Prime Minister, has made his vision clear; he seeks to raise Pakistan from the cusp of failure as a state and he seeks to realize the full potential of Pakistan. This year at CBMUN’19 Imran Khan’s Economic advisory council will place the burden of reviving Pakistan in the hands of the delegates. The delegates will work as the current economic advisors to Imran Khan, they’ll be challenged with the arduous task of strategizing, formulating and executing a fool proof plan to navigate Pakistan through the obstacles of Eco-Financial Crisis and its horde of Institutional corruption.

CWM

Governments often form a coalition government when in a state of war called the Cabinet of War. A similar cabinet was established by Winston Churchill on 10th May 1940, this was called Churchill’s War Ministry. This is a specially designed committee for the delegates whom will experience a carefully crafted environment simulating World-War II scenarios. As being amongst the members of Churchill’s War Ministry, the delegates will be burdened with task of defeating Nazis while expertly maintain cooperation with the allied nations.
